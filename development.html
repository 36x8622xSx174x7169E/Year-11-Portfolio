1.Body Proportion Sketch

Development

I used a 8 head proportional guide to draw my game character on, this means that the body parts are all the correct size in comparison to each other. I drew a side and front view of the character before scanning the final drawing and importing this into blender. My game character is modeled to be adventurous with a hood and a shoulderpad. After importing my drawing scans into blender I started building the character model starting with the torso then moving on to the arms,legs and then head. After this i did some basic detailing to give the character a hood, if I had had more time i would have loved to learn and incorperate some cloth simulaitons for a cloak and some of the clothing but i had to make do. After the basic modeling I moved onto animating first I started off by building the armature and parenting everything to the spine before linking each bone to the corresponding body part I started off with a default pose before moving onto an idle animation, this was the character bobbing up and down and the arms moving out and in. Later I started with the run animation this took me a while as i ended up reding the bones in the leg to fix a problem i was having to do this i watched an online totorial. I finished off by expanding on the running animation and adding a celebratory jump/pose at the end.

2. Task Two

Testing

I tested my animation muiltiple times during and after I made it. I made sure that the loop was clean and there were no sudden movements or unintended movements. I ended up testing the legs over 30 times duting the animation prosess due to a reoccuring issue where they were bending outwards to fix this i changed the bone structure in the legs'(ilater found out that this could be fixed by moving the knee joint closer towards the fromt of the knee)'. I also tested muiltiple different bone configurations in the arm including a modified version of that of the legs. In the end i stayed with the original as it provided the largest variety of movement needed. After rigourous testing i got to a point to where my animation looked good enough however i was unable to fix the issue which caused the animation to look floaty.

3. Task Three

Feedback

My Main feedback was from Robert, Charlie and my teacher Rachael The main feedback was about the legs of my character being too short, and that my character should be semeterical for easy mirroring and rigging. I fixed both of these issues.
